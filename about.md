I&rsquo;m **Chong Tze Zhao**, a final-year Computer Science student at the

methodology behind the work.

## What I build

A self-directed, end-to-end systematic-trading research stack: raw tick
ingestion and market-microstructure features, an
[AFML](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Advances+in+Financial+Machine+Learning-p-9781119482086)-style
machine-learning validation pipeline running on an HPC cluster, and a live
execution bot that routes orders off real signals. Research, methodology,
production, and live operation — the full path, not just a notebook.

## How I work

The through-line across everything I build is that **I&rsquo;m structurally
afraid of fooling myself, and I build the apparatus that proves it.** I
calibrate slippage from real fills, audit relentlessly for lookahead leakage,
and run the tests that are capable of killing my own ideas — purged/embargoed
cross-validation, honest transaction costs, sham controls. I&rsquo;ve killed
more strategies than I&rsquo;ve shipped, with evidence, because they
didn&rsquo;t survive honest cost. The one that did is running live.

That habit — *the work was right; the answer is just &ldquo;no&rdquo;* — is what
I want to write about here.
