I’m a final-year Computer Science student at the National University of Singapore, heading into quantitative research and research engineering. For the last few months I’ve built an end-to-end systematic-trading research stack — from raw tick ingestion and market-microstructure features, through an AFML-style ML validation pipeline on an HPC cluster, out to a live execution bot.

The thread through all of it: I’m structurally afraid of fooling myself, and I build the apparatus that proves it. I calibrate slippage from real fills, audit relentlessly for leakage, and run the tests that can kill my own ideas. I’ve killed more strategies than I’ve shipped — with evidence — because they didn’t survive honest cost. The one that did is running live.

I’m looking for a seat where that rigor is the job: quant researcher, quant developer, or research engineer.

Selected work

Self-directed crypto research, Feb–Jun 2026. Every result is grounded in a dated, reproducible artifact; numbers are cost, leakage, and infra facts — no signals or edge.

  • The phantom-fill trap

    A backtest is only as honest as its fill model — so I rebuild fills from raw order-book data before trusting any P&L.

    An Avellaneda–Stoikov market-maker backtested at Sharpe −3.5. I suspected the engine was filling on transient price-touches, rebuilt the fill model from order-book-delta-inferred trades, and showed ~65% of the fills were phantom. The strategy was never the problem — the simulation was lying.

    • microstructure
    • hftbacktest
    • Python

  • 269 commits, and the answer was “no”

    Most edges die at realistic cost — I model it from real fills and walk away from what doesn’t survive, with a reproducible paper trail.

    An ML-gated volatility-breakout strategy that looked clean on paper. A numba slippage model calibrated from 700+ real fills, plus a self-inflicted lookahead leak I caught, flipped the edge negative across all 42 parameter cells. I killed the strategy class and shipped a reproducible negative-result package.

    • backtesting
    • numba
    • walk-forward

  • The corruption detective

    When a model won’t learn, the data is guilty until proven innocent — I audit the panel before I ever tune the model.

    A model abstaining on every fold — so I audited the data, not the model. A streaming pass over 3 years of exchange archives found 33% non-monotonic timestamps and 13M+ duplicate trades inflating volatility features. I patched the panels and published both the contaminated and corrected results.

    • data-integrity
    • PyArrow
    • pandas

  • An AFML pipeline on HPC

    I build the validation machinery itself — with leakage gates enforced by the pipeline, not by good intentions.

    CUSUM event sampling, triple-barrier labels, purged/embargoed CV, sample weights, and a non-negotiable lookahead auditor gating every stage — deployed to a production config on a SLURM cluster that held up under 16 bps round-trip cost stress.

    • LightGBM
    • SLURM
    • AFML

  • Memory-leak archaeology

    Research only counts if it runs — I keep long-running data systems alive in production at scale.

    An unbounded leak in a 311-WebSocket async collector, traced past Python’s tracked allocator to PyArrow C++ column metadata in the glibc heap. Three fixes cut RSS drift from 2.4 GB to ~0 over a 104-hour run.

    • asyncio
    • systems
    • glibc
